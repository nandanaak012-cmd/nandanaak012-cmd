📚 The Basics

What Is a Deepfake?

No tech jargon — just plain English

🤥

What It Is

A deepfake is a photo or video where a computer has been used to make someone look or sound like they did or said something they never actually did. It's like a very convincing lie made with technology.

⚠️

Why It's Dangerous

Deepfakes can be used to spread false information, damage reputations, or manipulate elections. You might see a video of a celebrity saying something shocking — but it was never real.

🛡️

How We Help

TruthLens checks the tiny details hidden in every image and video that the human eye can't see. If something looks off, we flag it and explain why — in words you understand.

🧠 What We Look For

The Signs That Give It Away

These are the clues we check — explained simply

👁️

Unnatural Eyes

Fake videos often have eyes that don't blink naturally, or both eyes aren't perfectly aligned with the face.

High Risk Signal
💡

Wrong Lighting

Light and shadows on the face don't match the background — like a face lit from the left but shadows going right.

High Risk Signal
📸

Blurry Edges

The edges around hair, ears, or glasses look slightly blurry or melted, especially when the person moves.

Medium Risk Signal
🔢

Hidden File Data

Every real photo has hidden info (like which camera took it). If that info is missing or changed, it's suspicious.

High Risk Signal
🎭

Face Doesn't Match Voice

In videos, the mouth movements may not perfectly match the words being spoken — a common deepfake giveaway.

High Risk Signal
🔁

Repeated Patterns

Computer-made images often accidentally repeat tiny patterns in backgrounds or skin texture that real photos don't have.

Medium Risk Signal
